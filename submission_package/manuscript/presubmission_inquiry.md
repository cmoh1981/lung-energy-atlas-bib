Subject: Pre-submission inquiry for Briefings in Bioinformatics

Dear Editor,

We are writing to ask whether Briefings in Bioinformatics would consider a manuscript currently titled "From Cell Atlases to Radiogenomics: A Lung Energy Metabolism Case Study in Bioinformatics Validation Failure Modes." The article is framed as a review with worked case study that combines a general validation framework for atlas-guided radiogenomics with a detailed example based on the Lung Energy Metabolism Pathway Atlas (LEMPA).

This is explicitly not a pure original research report. Our aim is to provide a practitioner-oriented bioinformatics guide to validation design, failure-mode interpretation, and transparent reporting when atlas-derived cellular targets are linked to patient-scale imaging data. The LEMPA case study is used as the evidence anchor for that framework rather than as a stand-alone success claim.

The worked example is useful because the frozen empirical line remained negative and method-fragile under current public paired NSCLC resources. Instead of treating that outcome as a weakness to hide, the manuscript uses it to identify failure modes that practitioners need to document: deconvolution disagreement, target rarity and collapse, cohort linkage attrition, limited robustness surfaces, and the danger of overinterpreting narrow residual associations. The paper's central message is therefore methodological and field-facing: atlas-guided radiogenomics requires explicit validation gates before strong translational claims are made.

If this framing is within scope for Briefings in Bioinformatics, the manuscript would be submitted as a practitioner resource that offers three linked contributions: a general validation framework, a failure-mode taxonomy and reporting discipline, and a detailed case study showing how transparent negative closure can still produce reusable bioinformatics guidance. We would be grateful to know whether such a review/case-study format would be of potential interest to the journal.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
