# Executive Decision

Final classification: `METHOD-FRAGILE NEGATIVE`.

Executed fact: V1 remained negative, V2 remained analyzable but failed the key robustness and performance gates, and no new evidence in the post-V2 audit overturned that state.

Interpretation: the broad radiomics-to-cell-fraction validation line should be archived rather than continued as an active validation narrative.

Future work: only a narrow H4b heterogeneity sub-question may remain alive, and only as a caveated association-level remainder under the Task B verdict `H4B_INTERPRETABLE_WITH_MAJOR_CAVEATS`; Phase C should otherwise be held pending qualifying external resources.

# Evidence Basis

Executed fact: V1 was an honest negative or underpowered baseline, with only 3 cell types meeting the pre-registered B3 threshold and no BH-FDR-significant H4-family support in the frozen V1 outputs.

Executed fact: V2 ended with final classification `METHOD-FRAGILE NEGATIVE` after successful executability preflight but failed the intended deconvolution validation path.

Executed fact: multi-method agreement failed in V2 (`V2-G2 FAIL`), with top-10 NNLS-abundant agreement median `-0.087` and only `1/10` cell types at `r >= 0.4`, which triggered fallback to four broad modules.

Executed fact: B3-v2 then failed at module level (`V2-G4 FAIL`), with only `1/4` primary modules reaching pooled Pearson `r >= 0.30`, and cross-source robustness was not estimable from frozen local strata (`V2-G5 SKIPPED` / omitted).

Executed fact: the inherited H4-family table was populated in V2, and H4b remained the only positive-looking remainder while H4a and H4c stayed null.

Interpretation: the retained H4b signal is not sufficient to reverse the negative line-level outcome because it sits inside a run with failed multi-method agreement, failed module-level predictive validation, and no independent replication surface.

Executed fact from Task B: H4b was reproducible from the frozen V2 tables and did not appear to be caused by direct radiomic leakage or duplicate patient expansion, but the target definition was materially dependent on the V2 module-collapse fallback and was strengthened relative to the original V1 target definition.

# Interpretation Boundaries

Executed fact: there is no external replication cohort in the completed V1/V2 line.

Executed fact: there is no method-robust cell-type-level deconvolution result, because the intended cell-type agreement gate failed before downstream interpretation.

Executed fact: there is no positive B3 validation result, either at original cell-type resolution or at the collapsed module level.

Interpretation: H4b should be treated as association-only, not as validation of radiogenomic prediction, not as confirmation of cellular deconvolution accuracy, and not as evidence that the broader line succeeded.

Interpretation: because the V2 H4b target is variance over the collapsed four-module consensus rather than the unchanged V1 weighted-capacity heterogeneity target, any retained H4b language must stay explicitly subordinate to the negative primary outcome.

# Recommended Next State

Interpretation: archive the broad radiomics-to-cell-fraction validation line.

Future work: keep only a narrow H4b heterogeneity sub-question alive, and only in constrained internal language that states the result is reproducible within frozen V2 artifacts but carries major caveats tied to target redefinition, module collapse, and lack of external replication.

Future work: do not reopen the broad line by default. Phase C should remain deferred unless a validated independent paired imaging plus RNA-seq resource becomes available and is strong enough to test deconvolution robustness and pathway/radiomics relationships without relying on the current fallback structure.

# Do Not Claim

- No successful radiogenomic validation.
- No external replication.
- No method-robust cell-type prediction.
- No positive B3 validation at module level.
- No claim that H4b validates the project or rescues the failed line.
- No Warburg or glycolysis imaging support from H4a or H4c.
