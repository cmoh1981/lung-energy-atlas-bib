Practitioner Checklist — Atlas-Guided Radiogenomics
5 Action Items Before Running Your Paired Analysis
1
Freeze validation gates before data exploration. Pre-specify agreement threshold (e.g., median r ≥ 0.0), correlation gate (e.g., r ≥ 0.30), and robustness expectations. Record in analysis plan before paired data exploration.
2
Test deconvolution agreement first. Run at least two independent methods (NNLS + scaden or equivalent). Do not proceed to single-method predictive modeling if top-10 median agreement is negative.
3
Prefer abundant, stable targets over biologically specific ones. Rare cell-type endpoints fail more often than broad module endpoints. If your primary target is rare, declare a fallback module-level target in advance.
4
Define a fallback hypothesis for residual associations. If the primary gate fails, residual associations (like H4b) are only interpretable if the target was pre-specified as a fallback. Do not retrofit post-hoc.
5
Report negative outcomes as first-class results. A transparent METHOD-FRAGILE NEGATIVE is more useful to the field than an optimistic reframe of a failed primary gate. Document failure modes explicitly.

Five decision nodes define whether a study should continue to predictive evaluation, collapse to module-level targets, defer pending a larger cohort, or archive the line entirely. LEMPA encountered failure at Nodes 3 (G2 partial: NNLS-only consensus) and 5 (1/4 modules passed the pooled CV threshold), resulting in a METHOD-FRAGILE NEGATIVE line classification. The practitioner checklist summarizes five pre-analysis actions that reduce the risk of overclaiming and enable transparent negative-result reporting. H4b = hypothesis 4b (texture-versus-heterogeneity association). CV = cross-validation. FDR = Benjamini-Hochberg false discovery rate.
